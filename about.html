ABOUT :


The jellyfish species known as Atlantic sea nettle (Chrysaora quinquecirrha), or East Coast sea nettle in the US, is found along the country's Atlantic coast. It was once mistaken for other Chrysaora species, leading to inaccurate reporting.


To grasp food, it has tentacles all around its mouth. There are no respiratory or excretory organs on sea nettles. Every sea nettle is either in a polyp stage or a stage when it may swim freely. The polyp stage reproduces asexually, while the free-swimming stage, sometimes known as the medusa stage, reproduces sexually.


The Atlantic sea nettle is a bell-shaped invertebrate that has reddish-brown stripes and tiny white spots. It is often semi-transparent. The bell of sea nettles without stripes is opaque or appears white.


FEEDING HABITS :


Plankton and other marine invertebrates are eaten by carnivorous compass jellyfish.
Dinoflagellates, copepods, crab eggs, larval fish, and chaetognaths are just a few of the benthic and pelagic creatures they consume.
Their tentacles are covered in stinging cells that paralyze and catch their prey.
Captured prey can enter the oral aperture more easily thanks to the oral arms.
There aren't many predators of compass jellyfish.
It is known that ocean sunfish and leatherback sea turtles eat them.

Carnivorous plants are stinging sea nettles. Generally, they eat other jellies, ctenophores, zooplankton, and occasionally crustaceans.
With their stinging tentacles, sea nettles paralyze and capture their prey. Next, the prey is sent to the stomach, where it is broken down for consumption.

In addition, sea nettles may eat mosquito larvae, worms, minnows, and eggs from bay anchovies.

LIFE CYCLE


Chrysaora hysoscella, like other Scyphozoans, goes through metamorphosis as it grows, first taking the shape of a polyp and then a medusa. Planular larvae released by females swim about in search of a good area to settle. The planulae cling to a benthic substrate and grow into a sessile polyp known as strobilation, which is an asexual reproductive process that releases juvenile medusae. Chrysaora hysoscella is a prototypically hermaphrodite creature that matures as a male before developing female gametes.
